Chunking
Chunking strategy  : Hierarchical  structural chunking 

Unlike "Fixed-Size Chunking" (which cuts text every X words), Hierarchical Chunking breaks the CMS manuals down based on their natural document structure: Manual → Chapter → Section → Subsection. Each "chunk" of text is stored alongside its parent headers as metadata.

Chunking strategy: 
Primary split: by section heading ( detected via fontsize / regex on headings like 10 .1, 20. 2)
Target chunk size: -500 tokens
Overlap: -50 tokens (for sentence continuity) - 
Each chunk carries metadata:
{manual_id, chapter_num, chapter_title, section_title, page_num,
Source_url}
Why it is Required
In healthcare policy, context defines the rule. CMS manuals are highly nested; a single sentence about "coverage limits" is meaningless unless the system knows it falls under Chapter 7: Home Health Services and Section 40.1: Skilled Nursing Requirements.
Hierarchical chunking is required because:
Context Preservation: It ensures that every small snippet of text "remembers" which policy it belongs to.
Multi-Level Retrieval: It allows the AI to look at a specific paragraph (the "child") while staying aware of the broader section rules (the "parent").
Patient Safety: In a medical context, providing a "Part A" rule to a patient asking about "Part B" is a critical error. Hierarchy prevents this mix-up.
