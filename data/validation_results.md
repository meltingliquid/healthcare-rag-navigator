### ❓ QUESTION:
> What four conditions must all be met for care in a skilled nursing facility to be covered under Medicare?

### 💡 GROUND TRUTH ANSWER:
> Medicare covers SNF care when all four of the following are met: (1) the patient requires skilled nursing services or skilled rehabilitation services that must be performed by or under the supervision of professional or technical personnel; (2) the patient requires these skilled services for a condition that was treated during a qualifying inpatient hospital stay, or for a condition that arose while in the SNF for treatment of such a condition; (3) the patient requires these skilled services on a daily basis (essentially 7 days per week); and (4) as a practical matter considering economy and efficiency, the daily skilled services can only be provided on an inpatient basis in a SNF. Ref: 100-02, Ch.8, §30.

### ❓ QUESTION:
> What establishes medical necessity for Medicare to cover ambulance transport?

### 💡 GROUND TRUTH ANSWER:
> Medical necessity for ambulance transport is established when: (1) the patient's condition at the time of transport is such that other means of transportation are contraindicated; and (2) any means of transport other than an ambulance would be medically contraindicated or would endanger the patient's health or safety. The medical necessity determination is based on the individual patient's condition at the time of the transport, not on the diagnosis alone. Documentation must be available upon request to support this determination; a physician's order alone is not sufficient proof of medical necessity. Ref: 100-02, Ch.10, §10.2.1.

### ❓ QUESTION:
> When is a patient's place of residence considered ineligible for Medicare home health services?

### 💡 GROUND TRUTH ANSWER:
> A patient cannot receive Medicare home health services if they reside in an institution that itself qualifies as a Medicare provider, such as a hospital, SNF, or other participating institution. If a patient remains in a participating SNF following discharge from active care, the facility may not be considered their residence and they are not entitled to payment for home health services under either Part A or Part B. Medicare coverage is also not an optional substitute for services that a facility is required by law to provide to its patients or that are included in the facility's base contract. An individual's choice to reside in such a facility is also a choice to accept the services it holds itself out as providing. Ref: 100-02, Ch.7, §30.1.2.

### ❓ QUESTION:
> What face-to-face encounter requirements must be met for a physician to certify a patient for home health services?

### 💡 GROUND TRUTH ANSWER:
> A certifying physician or allowed practitioner must have had a face-to-face encounter with the patient no more than 90 days prior to the home health start of care date, or within 30 days after the start of care. If home health care is ordered for a new condition not evident during a visit within 90 days prior to start of care, the physician or a non-physician practitioner (NPP) must see the patient again within 30 days after admission. The encounter must be related to the primary reason for home health services. NPPs performing the encounter are subject to the same financial restrictions with the HHA as the certifying physician. ### ❓ QUESTION:
> What home health nursing services involving medication administration are not covered under Medicare?

### 💡 GROUND TRUTH ANSWER:
> The administration of eye drops and topical ointments does not require the skills of a nurse and is therefore not covered as a skilled nursing service, even if the administration is necessary for the treatment of an illness or injury, the patient cannot self-administer, and there is no one else available to administer them. Additionally, the administration of oral medications by a nurse is generally not a covered skilled nursing service unless the complexity of the patient's condition, the nature of the drugs, and the number of drugs prescribed require the skills of a licensed nurse to detect and evaluate side effects or reactions, which must be documented in the medical record. Ref: 100-02, Ch.7, §40.1.2.4.
